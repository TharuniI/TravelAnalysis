Figure 4: Why You Should Visit a Country

Description: This is graph uses facet_wrap to figure out the correlation between different factors and the number of visits a country gets.
Datasets: Most Visited Countries of the World, Cost of Living Index, World Crime Index, World Sanitation & Health

Findings: This graph tells us that living costs, safety, and the overall sanitation level play role in whether people visit a country or not. For living cost, we can see most of the data residing to the left. This means that lower costs is corrleated to higher travel rates. Countries in the mid-range on the safety index seem to have the highest travel rate. Finally, we can see that a country must be very clean for higher travel rates.
